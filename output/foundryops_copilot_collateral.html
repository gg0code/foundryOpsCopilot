The pour, with a second pair of eyes.

The operator at the ladle is the only person who can still change the outcome of a heat. Once the metal moves, the next decision point is shakeout — hours later, and far too expensive to undo. FoundryOps Copilot puts the defect risk, the parameter driving it, and the corrective action on a single screen, minutes before tap. Not a dashboard. A decision aid, at the only moment a decision is still possible.

The same screen serves four readers. The shift operator sees a red/green gauge, the most-likely defect, and three corrective actions. The quality engineer sees the SHAP attribution that names the driver. The metallurgist sees the chemistry and physics chain traced to IS 210 and AFS references. The plant head sees the rupee impact per casting and the annualised exposure. One model, one screen, four readings.

Why ZeroZeta 03 / 03
Why us — the honest version

Most foundry software is built for the audit binder and the month-end review. Ours is built for the four minutes before the metal moves.

01

Predictive, not retrospective

MES dashboards and SAP quality modules tell you what scrapped yesterday. FoundryOps Copilot tells you what's about to scrap this heat — while pour temp, delay, sand moisture, and inoculant dose are still adjustable. Prevented at the ladle, not reconciled at shakeout.

02

Every prediction is explained

Conservative foundry buyers don't trust black boxes, and they're right not to. Every defect prediction ships with its top six SHAP drivers in the operator's vocabulary: pour delay in minutes, humidity in percent, pattern age in cycles. If the metallurgist disagrees with the model, the argument lives on the same page as the answer.

03

Rupees, not just percentages

A 76% defect risk persuades no one in a CFO review. Expected loss per casting — split into scrap, rework, delay, OEM complaint, and warranty reserve, each with a published formula and a calibrated unit cost — turns a model output into a P&L line the plant head can defend at month-end.

04

Built for your plant, not a textbook

Off-the-shelf models trained on academic datasets assume a steady-state plant that doesn't exist in India. FoundryOps Copilot retrains on your heat logs — your operators, your furnaces, your patterns, your monsoon. Seasonal effects, pattern wear past 800 cycles, furnace lining drift — learned from your data, not assumed from a paper.
